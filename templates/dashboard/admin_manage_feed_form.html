What is a Feed Schedule? A feed schedule is your daily feeding plan for a species — how much feed they eat, how many times a day, what it costs you, and which season it applies to. Once saved, the system automatically works out your daily feed cost and shows it in your livestock analytics.

How the system uses this
1
You enter: feed type, kg/day, cost per kg, feeding times.
2
System calculates: Daily cost = daily kg × cost per kg. Shown live above.
3
Analytics page shows a bar chart of feed costs per species — so you always know where your money goes.
4
Seasonal flag lets you create a dry-season and a rainy-season schedule separately if feeding amounts differ.
5
Staff members can see feeding schedules on their dashboard and know exactly when and what to feed.
Quick Tips
  • For Layer hens, typical feeding is 3× daily: morning, noon, evening.
  • For Catfish, once or twice daily is standard — morning and late afternoon.
  • For Broilers, feed is usually available all day (ad libitum) — enter feeding times as All day.
  • You can add multiple schedules for the same species — e.g. one for dry season, one for rainy season.
